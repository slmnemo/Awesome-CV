  \tab Hello, I am an EIT-eligible electrical engineer with a degree in general engineering.
  In the past, I have worked as an electrical firmware engineer focused on FPGAs, and I'm hoping to
  pivot into infrastructure for a myriad of reasons. This work is interesting to me because
  I want to work in a field where my work directly improves the lives of those around me.
  From a technical perspective, working with and designing using low, medium, and 
  high voltage equipment for people to use is an interesting challenge and is satisfying to me.
  Concord Engineering caught my interest as one such firm focused on traffic engineering, as I appreciate
  the work that goes into making a street usable for different modes of transportation. Something notable from
  the posting was that you also design EV charging station's power distribution, which is another interest of mine as an EV owner.

  \tab As a former silicon and aerospace engineer, I bring with me an attention to detail and a strong work ethic.
  I have worked on both a camera system for a satellite and on a computer chip capable of running Linux at previous
  positions which both required an attention to detail, and I think this attention to detail will be critical working
  in utilities as well. As a result of working in these positions, I have also become highly proficient in working with 
  computers, a skill that I hope will continue to be helpful within this sector. Within my duties of work, I have also had to
  conduct customer meetings, meet deadlines, and work in a team; skills which are transferrable to any industry.
  
  \tab In addition to my computer proficiency and attention to detail, I bring with me a strong understanding of electrical 
  systems, as I primarily studied electrical classes in college during my general engineering degree, and many of my 
  jobs have involved testing at the edge of electrical and computer interfaces. At my previous positions, I was involved 
  in the testing of multiple electrical interfaces related to radios, custom cameras, and medical devices. This electrical 
  experience has influenced me to lean more into the electrical sector rather than continue on with computers, and has 
  given me insight into mitigating EMI in devices and characterizing the frequency response of different systems. I hope 
  that my ability to learn new things is considered for engineering positions at Concord Engineering, as I am eager to learn quickly.

\end{cvletter}
